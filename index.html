Workforce Augmentation

Workforce Augmentation
for Your Institute

During our ongoing engagements within Defence, Government and large scale corporate organisations, we have consistently identified a failure of recruitment agencies, consulting organisations and suppliers to correctly analyse the requirements for individual roles and larger workforce teams. This failure invariably leads to poor outcomes for the client, high turnover within the workforce, and has a detrimental impact on the individuals placed into roles that were outside their skill and experience.

With our extensive experience in the analysis and design of organisational workforce solutions, ACG is uniquely placed to support clients in scoping individual and team capability requirements, and subsequently sourcing and managing high quality specialists who are accurately matched to their roles based on their skills, knowledge, experience and aptitude. We provide an end-to-end service which includes:

  • Supporting the client to define and scope the requirement
  • Sourcing and rigorously screening potential candidates
  • assisting clients in reviewing, shortlisting, and selection of candidates
  • supporting both the client and candidate through the onboarding process to support seamless integration and reduce the learning curve involved in commencing a new role or function.

ACG is also unique in that we provide an ongoing career management function for staff encompassing welfare, professional development, and career guidance. We take pride in establishing long term relationships with high calibre staff that can provide ongoing value to our clients and customers.

Workforce Analysis and Development

Workforce Analysis and Development
for Your Institute

Audacity Consulting specialises in the analysis of workforce solutions for clients, whether they are Government Departments, large corporate providers, or Not-for-Profit (NFP) organisations.

Our specialists work with clients across all phases of workforce development and commonly include the following support services:

  • Workforce analysis. Our consultants will conduct a detailed analysis of the workforce required to support an organisation, department, project or platform to accurately identify staff numbers, capabilities, proficiency requirements and training parameters. This can include requirements to support individual projects or may support workforce optimisation and restructure to gain efficiencies in cost, productivity or change. Our analysis projects are commonly requested by organisations seeking to understand the implications of new systems or capabilities, or those requiring restructure to support operational goals.
  • Training Needs Analysis. ACG has many years of experience using our vocational education team to analyse the training needs of major organisations to enhance efficiency, meet regulatory requirements, or to more effectively understand and deliver professional development programs against staff and business needs. Our TNA process results in a comprehensive analysis of the current state of training against optimised outcomes and provides detailed recommendations for training with comprehensive implementation guidelines. Additionally, with extensive experience in the design and delivery of training, our team can support the development of training programs and can even facilitate programs or provide Train-the-Trainer workshops where required.

Offshore Resourcing

Offshore Resourcing
for Your Institute

As part of our comprehensive workforce support services, Audacity Consulting also assists organisations to establish offshore support services in order to enhance their existing capabilities, or to offer workforce efficiencies against cost, productivity and capacity. Our consultants have worked extensively with Business Process Outsourcing (BPO) services and have operated large scale support teams across services including administration, marketing, financial management and customer service.

Audacity is not a BPO and as such we do not directly hire the offshore staff on your behalf. Our role as a workforce consulting company is remove the risk of outsourcing and to assist you in establishing and operating a highly proficient and professional support capability with a reputable offshore service provider.

We will guide you through the process of:

  • Analysing your business to identify areas that may be outsourced.
  • Establishing the outsourcing model including development of position descriptions, mapping tasks and processes to develop Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs), establishing communications and management processes, and introducing the technology required to enable effective offshoring.
  • Reviewing potential service providers and guiding you through their service offerings, pricing models and contract frameworks including identification of hidden costs and fees.
  • Screening and engaging staff provided by your provider.
  • Onboarding and training your new support team.
  • Training your staff to work with and manage remote, offshore staff effectively.

This is always a good place to start—consulting is a vague field to the average person. Therefore, many people might not even realize that they need or could benefit from consulting services. This is your chance to educate them and to show off your skills. If people don’t know what a consultant is or does, how can they know that they need one? It might seem like a simple question to you, an industry expert, but to the average user, it’s a big question that gets huge hits in search engine results. Explain all the details of what you do, what you don’t do, and give people room to ask more questions if they’d like. This is a great place to encourage people to reach out to you via email, live chat, or another means of communication to ask questions and discuss their specific needs.

First, note that we ask “why”, not “if”—this eliminates the chance for people to think they don’t need a business consultant. Use this question to point out all the perks that come from working with a consultant and help people see what value you have to offer for their business. SMBs, especially, can stand to gain a lot of valuable insight because they may not be as familiar with the consulting industry as others in business. This question gives you a chance to sell your services and show people all that you can do. It also allows you to inform them of just what a business consultant does.

This is a question on everyone’s list. People like to know prices, even when they don’t realize it’s something that can’t just be quoted outright. Sure, there are “average” rates for consultants, and you can use this area to discuss what your rates are or how much the average business spends on consulting, but you will never be able to give people a locked-in price. The point here is to be transparent about your pricing and make sure that people understand how you charge more so than what you charge (plus, that’s another question we’ll cover in a minute). Your explanation of the pricing and variables in estimating costs is more valuable than the actual numbers, anyway.

Businesses in need of help often find themselves struggling to figure out what coaches and consultants do, and what makes them different from each other. After all, at first glance, they seem like they offer similar services. However, that’s far from reality. Coaches are trained to actively coach businesses on how to become successful and build a better company. They also coach the leader(s) of the business on how to be confident and improve personally to help their professional career. A consultant is more of an advisory type of person—they will consult on various processes and practices within the business to help make improvements and will generally not offer a lot of personal advice or focus on personal growth as much as the business itself. In either case, both can benefit a business in the right situation.

This is a big point of contention in the industry—most people will offer at least a phone call or quick chat without a charge, but some balk at the idea entirely. However, if you do offer some type of free consult to your clients or potential clients, you could gain a lot more business than you might expect. Be sure to explain exactly what you can do for people and how you can assist them, and then discuss what your fees will be if they decide to continue working with you. When you focus on problem-solving more than pricing, so will they.
